The CO₂ from these sources adds to the natural cycle of CO₂ in the atmosphere, but human activities have dramatically increased the concentration of CO₂, especially since the Industrial Revolution.

### Enhanced Greenhouse Effect:
As more greenhouse gases accumulate in the atmosphere:
1. **Increased Absorption**: More CO₂ absorbs more infrared radiation.
2. **Retained Heat**: This trapped heat is re-emitted in all directions, including back to the Earth's surface.
3. **Warming**: This leads to a greater average temperature on Earth, known as global warming.

### Feedback Loops:
- **Melting Ice**: As the Earth warms, ice melts, exposing darker surfaces that absorb more heat, further warming the planet.
- **Water Vapor**: Warmer air can hold more water vapor, which is another potent greenhouse gas. This amplifies the warming effect.

This enhanced greenhouse effect leads to a rise in global temperatures, causing various climatic changes like more frequent heatwaves, shifts in weather patterns, and rising sea levels.

- **Water Vapor Increase**: Higher temperatures lead to more evaporation, increasing water vapor in the atmosphere. Water vapor is a powerful greenhouse gas, trapping even more heat.
- **Example**: Warm air can hold more water vapor, creating a cycle where warming leads to more warming.

### Other Feedback Mechanisms:
- **Melting Permafrost**: As permafrost thaws, it releases stored carbon dioxide and methane, both potent greenhouse gases. This is another positive feedback loop that increases warming.
- **Deforestation**: Trees absorb CO₂, but deforestation reduces this sink. Without trees to absorb CO₂, more CO₂ stays in the atmosphere, further enhancing the greenhouse effect.
