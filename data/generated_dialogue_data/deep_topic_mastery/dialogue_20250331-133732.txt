[Assistant] Sure! Cells can be broadly classified into two main types: **prokaryotic cells** and **eukaryotic cells**. 

- **Prokaryotic Cells**: These are typically simpler and smaller, lacking a membrane-bound nucleus. They include bacteria and archaea. Prokaryotic cells have a single circular chromosome and no organelles like mitochondria.

- **Eukaryotic Cells**: These have a membrane-bound nucleus and organelles, making them more complex. They include plant, animal, fungal, and protist cells. Eukaryotic cells have structures like mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, and a Golgi apparatus, each with specific functions.

For example, plant cells have chloroplasts for photosynthesis, while animal cells don't have chloroplasts but have structures like the mitochondria for energy production.
